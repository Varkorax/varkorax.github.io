Milky Way Bar Crawl - Introduction

Welcome!

▾

Welcome to a galactic bar crawl featuring my curated selection of mods!

In this guide I will walk you through how to completely overhaul the Mass Effect Legendary Edition - aimed at making the trilogy feel more immersive, cohesive, and alive for both first-time players and long-time veterans.

Of course, this is only one of many ways to experience the trilogy. Mass Effect modding isn’t as complex as, say, Skyrim’s, but I still believe there’s real value in a clear, structured guide - especially for steps that veterans may take for granted.


A little about me:
I’ve been playing Mass Effect since 2012. Between the original releases and the Legendary Edition, I’ve completed each game more than 20 times. I started modding around 2018 and haven’t played without mods since. This list has grown and evolved alongside my own playthroughs - tested, refined, and shaped by countless nights spent tweaking my setup.

Structure

▸

We’ll begin with a general Setup to prepare your games, folders, and the mod manager.

After that, the guide splits into installing mods for each of the three games in order.

Finally, since texture mods should always be installed last, they’re grouped under the After Hours section - along with a few optional extras for those who want to go a bit further.

Terminology

▸

A quick primer on a few terms you'll see frequently while modding Mass Effect:

Term Full Description
OT Original Trilogy Refers to the original PC releases.
LE Legendary Edition Refers to this version of the games.
LE1 / LE2 / LE3 Legendary Edition (per game) Shorthand for a specific LE title.
M3 ME3Tweaks Mod Manager The standard tool in the Mass Effect modding scene.
MEM Mass Effect Modder Used to install and manage game textures.
Batch Install Group — A feature of M3 that installs a preconfigured list of mods.

Before We Begin

▸

Before we can get started, there are a few things you’ll need to know:

Version:
This guide is written for the English release of the game on Steam or the EA App.
Only Windows 10 64-bit (Non-LTSC) or newer is officially supported. It may run on Linux, but I can’t offer support for that setup.

Legal:
It should go without saying, but cracked or modified copies are not supported.

Performance:
Generally, if you can run the LE and get a stable framerate, you'll be fine.
My setup (NVIDIA RTX 2060 Super [8 GB VRAM], Ryzen 7 3700X, 16 GB RAM) is a touch older, but runs all three games fully modded at 1080p / 60 FPS.
I strongly recommend installing the game on an SSD to keep load times down.

Gameplay:
This guide was designed with Keyboard and Mouse in mind.
Not that controllers won't work, but I don't play that way so mileage may wary.

Additionally, there are a few things you’ll need to bring to the table:

Time:
This guide should take roughly one hour per game. Texture modding can extend this by another hour or more.

Attention:
Don’t skim. I wrote each step down for a reason - skipping can lead to issues later.

Basic PC familiarity:
Installing software, handling archives, navigating file paths, and light troubleshooting.

Disk space:
The Legendary Edition itself takes up ~110 GB.
Depending on your choices having ~200 GB free should cover most setups.
Modding all three games at once and/or storing all archives can push this closer to ~250 GB.

Archive tool:
You’ll need something to open compressed files. I recommend NanaZip.

Downloads:
All mods used here are hosted on Nexus Mods. You’ll need a free account to download them.



?

Caution: Free accounts are capped at 3 MB/s. Considering that some mods are quite large, a month of Premium may be worthwhile.
A few mods also offer torrent downloads as an alternative. If you choose that route, I recommend qBitTorrent.

Backup (Optional):
If you have spare storage, I highly recommend backing up your unmodded game folder.
More on this later.
